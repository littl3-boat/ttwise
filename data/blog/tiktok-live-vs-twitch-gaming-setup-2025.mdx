1. **Pin Down Your Priorities**: Ask yourself – do I want rapid-fire growth from shareable moments? TikTok Live nails that for phone-scrolling crowds. Or is it about forging lasting bonds with monetization perks like donations? That's Twitch territory. Experiment by mirroring content across both and noting engagement drops.

2. **Gear Up for Dual Streaming**: Snag OBS Studio; it's cost-free and endlessly tweakable. Build scenes including your gameplay, camera, and graphics. Link TikTok via built-in tools or services like Restream – no need to chase down elusive stream keys.

3. **Master the Capture Setup**: Link your console to the card. Drop it into OBS as a capture source. In TikTok's studio, opt for direct links over window grabs to dodge delays. Your computer's gotta keep up – think mid-range CPU and solid memory, or expect hiccups.

4. **Customize Per App**: Vertical format rules TikTok, spiced with polls for buzz. Switch to wide-screen on Twitch with integrated chat. Plan around audiences: prime time evenings for Twitch loyalists, flexible slots for TikTok's feed.

5. **Trial and Fix**: Launch a low-key test stream. Hunt for sync problems or freezes, gather input. Missing notifications? Revisit settings and software versions – it's usually that simple.

## FAQ / Notes
- **Ditching Streamlabs?** Commenters say yes, citing history issues; OBS offers better flexibility.
- **TikTok Key Troubles?** They can be elusive – rely on verified software or qualify for advanced access.
- **Earning Comparisons?** Twitch excels with structured subs; TikTok's gift system feels more unpredictable for games.

No need to commit to one platform – hit TikTok Live and Twitch together to amp up your gaming reach. Use these pointers to sidestep the Reddit-highlighted pitfalls and watch your following grow. Begin with basics, review data, adjust. Time to level up your streams!
