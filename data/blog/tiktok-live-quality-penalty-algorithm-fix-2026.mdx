## TL;DR

TikTok doesn't have a manual "quality penalty" button, but it does have **Ingest Trust Signals**. If your stream is too "heavy" (high bitrate/resolution) for the mobile-first ingest server, the algorithm stops pushing you to the FYP to prevent user buffering. To fix it, drop to **720p30 at 4000kbps** with a **2s Keyframe Interval** to regain the algorithm's "Stability Trust."

---

## The "High Quality" Paradox on TikTok Live

It’s the most common trap for experienced streamers moving from Twitch to TikTok: **Thinking that better quality equals more views.**

On TikTok, the opposite is often true. Because 99% of your viewers are on mobile devices (often on cellular data), a high-bitrate 1080p stream is a liability. If the algorithm detects that your data stream is "stuttering" or causing buffering for a test group of 5 viewers, it will immediately kill the push to the next 500.

This is what creators call a "Quality Penalty," but it's actually just a technical safety protocol.

## 3 Reasons Your "HD" Stream Gets 0 Views

### 1. The Bitrate "Red Line"
TikTok's ingest servers are optimized for mobile consumption. When you push 8,000kbps or higher, you're pushing the limits. If a viewer's connection can't keep up, the algorithm detects **"Viewer Drop (Technical)"** and assumes your stream is broken.
*   **Fix**: Stick to **3000 - 5000kbps**. Stability is more important than pixel-perfection.

### 2. Resolution Scaling Issues
On a 6-inch phone screen, 1080p has diminishing returns. However, the processing power required to decode it is high. If your stream causes a viewer's phone to heat up or stutter, they swipe away instantly.
*   **Fix**: Switch to **720p**. It feels "native" to TikTok Live and is much more reliable for triggering FYP pushes.

### 3. Keyframe Handshake Failure
This is the silent killer. If your Keyframe Interval isn't set to exactly **2 seconds**, TikTok's "Slicer" (the tech that turns your live feed into FYP clips) can't find the start of video segments.
*   **Result**: You are live, but invisible to the FYP recommendations.

## Action Checklist: Algorithm Reset

If your views are stuck at zero, run these settings for your next three streams to "reset" your quality score:

- [ ] **Resolution**: 720x1280 (Vertical) or 1280x720 (Horizontal)
- [ ] **Frame Rate**: 30 FPS (Better for mobile delivery than 60 FPS)
- [ ] **Bitrate**: 4000kbps CBR (Constant Bitrate)
- [ ] **Keyframe Interval**: 2s (Mandatory)
- [ ] **Encoder Preset**: 'Faster' or 'Very Fast' (Don't overload the encoder)

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a "Shadowban" for high quality?**
A: No. It's a "Stability Ban." If your stream is too heavy to load, TikTok won't show it. It’s a business decision to save bandwidth and improve user experience.

**Q: Can I go back to 1080p later?**
A: Yes, once you have a stable audience and high "Account Authority," but for discovery (0-100 viewers), 720p is the king of the FYP.

**Q: Does TikTok Live Studio work better than OBS?**
A: Not necessarily. It uses the same ingest servers. The settings (bitrate/keyframes) matter more than the software.
