## Introduction

In r/TikTokCreators, the most haunting post is: *"I hit 1k viewers today! But... only 3 people chatted. Is my content boring?"*

No. Your content is fine. The problem is **Friction**.

On TikTok, typing is hard.
- The keyboard covers half the screen.
- They are laying in bed with one hand.
- They are "passive scrollers" (TV Mode), not "active searchers" (PC Mode).

If you ask *"How is everyone doing today?"*, you are asking them to sit up, use two hands, and think of a sentence. That is too much work for a stranger.

## The Interaction Friction Ladder

To get chat moving, you must start at the bottom of the ladder (Low Friction) and work your way up.

### Level 1: The "Spam" Hook (Low Friction)
**Goal:** Get them to touch the keyboard *once*.
- **Don't ask:** "What do you guys think?"
- **Do ask:** "Type '1' if I should push, '2' if I should camp."
- **Why it works:** It requires zero brainpower and one finger tap.

### Level 2: The "Binary Choice" (Medium Friction)
**Goal:** Get them to express an opinion.
- **Do ask:** "Chat, are we feeling the Red outfit or the Blue one today?"
- **Why it works:** It limits the options. Paradox of choice is gone.

### Level 3: The "Open Question" (High Friction)
**Goal:** meaningful conversation.
- **Do ask:** "What's the best weapon for this boss?"
- **Rule:** Only ask this *after* the chat is already moving from Level 1 and 2. You cannot start a cold car in 5th gear.

## The Passive Scroller Mindset

You are fighting against biology. The "scroll" is a dopamine loop designed to be passive. You have to break that loop.

## 3 Psychological Triggers to Wake Them Up

### 1. The "Incorrect Statement" (Bait)
Nothing makes a lurker type faster than the urge to correct someone.
- **Say:** "I think this weapon is actually the best in the game." (When it's clearly mid).
- **Result:** 50 people typing "NOOOO it sucks."
- **Note:** Don't lie; just have a controversial (but harmless) opinion.

### 2. The "Subconscious Roll Call"
Don't say "Hello everyone." Say "Hello to the 500 people watching right now."
- Acknowledging the *number* makes them feel seen but not targeted. It breaks the "I'm invisible" wall.

### 3. The "Visual Interrupt"
If you are just gameplay, they will just watch.
- **Action:** Put a physical sticky note on your forehead. Or wear a weird hat.
- **Result:** "Why is there a note on your head?"
- **Why it works:** It breaks the visual pattern of the FYP.

## Actionable Checklist

- [ ] **Audit Your Questions:** Are you asking open-ended "How are you?" questions? Stop.
- [ ] **Switch to Binary:** Change every question to "This or That?"
- [ ] **Use the '1' in Chat:** Every 10 minutes, ask for a "1 in the chat" check.
- [ ] **Ignore the Silence:** Never say "Chat is dead." That kills the vibe instantly. Talk to the silence until it answers back.

## FAQ

**Q: Are they bots?**
A: Rarely. TikTok counts "previews" as views. If you have 500 viewers, 400 might be real people just scrolling by. They aren't bots; they are just fast.

**Q: Does 'Subscriber Only' chat kill engagement?**
A: Yes. Unless you have 10k+ viewers, turn that off. You need the lurkers to become chatters to become subscribers.

**Q: Why do they follow but not chat?**
A: Following is a "bookmark." Chatting is "socializing." They are bookmarking you for later. That is still a win.

## Practical Conclusion

Silence is not rejection. Silence is just inertia.
Your job as a streamer is not just to play the game; it is to be the **Interaction Engineer**. Lower the friction, give them easy buttons to press (Type '1'), and once the wheel starts turning, the conversation will follow.
